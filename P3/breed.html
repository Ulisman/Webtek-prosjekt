Why I love the GRIFFON BRUXELLOIS!

My family has, in earlier years, had a Bichon Havanese dog. His name was Zuku. The havanese are a lovely breed that are cuddly and nice. Although I loved Zuku, I must admit that Nala, who is a Griffon Bruxellois, is much easier to train, and picks up tricks way faster. The Griffon Bruxellois is such a nice breed for the family and is really recommended if you’re thinking of getting your first dog. This breed has a lot of nice traits that make it favorable for new dog owners. For one, it is extremely playful! I can spend hours on end throwing toys around and playing tug of war with Nala. She just never gets tired. The Griffon Bruxellois also has hair coat rather than fur coat. This means that they shed less and, therefore, enact less of an allergic reaction for those that are allergic. This is really nice if you often have friends over. Nala is also wonderful with guests and is never shy or aggressive when meeting new people.

However, I don’t think I would recommend this dog breed for families with very young children. The type of play that young children engage in can be interpreted as quite aggressive which may encourage the dog to respond with similar play. This can lead to unpredictable behavior and in some cases biting if antagonized although it is never with the intention of hurting you. I really love the breed and Nala has never caused such problems getting a dog is a big responsibility and it is important to consider the implications if you decide to get a dog. The breed is known for being a great family dog and has a bunch of energy for walks and play. Nala is again absolutely amazing and has been the absolute best throughout my experience with her. Hope you guys also share some of my joy by looking at the gallary of pictures I have of her. :)
